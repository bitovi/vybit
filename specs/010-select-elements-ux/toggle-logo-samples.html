VyBit Toggle — Eggburt Logo Concepts

Using the official Bitovi Eggburt mascot as the VyBit toggle button, with AI-glow treatments. Hover any button to see the rainbow eye glow. The button only lives on the page when the panel is closed — it disappears once the panel opens.

2 Option A — Full-Spectrum Rainbow Halo

A rotating conic-gradient ring spans the full visible spectrum. At idle the ring is subtle (‌50% opacity, 5-second rotation). On hover it brightens and speeds up. The button disappears (scale + fade) when the panel is opened — hover the "Active" button and click it to simulate this transition.

Recommendation: This is the strongest AI signal. The rotating spectrum ring is now widely recognized as the visual language of AI-powered tools (Copilot, Gemini, Cursor, ChatGPT). It immediately communicates "this is something smart" before the user interacts with the panel.
Idle → active: The ring slows to a gentle ambient rotation at idle (5s/rev). When hovered, it accelerates (2s/rev). When the panel opens, the button scales down and fades out completely — no need for an "active" visual state since the button is gone.
3 Option B — Brand Colors Glow (Teal ↔ Orange)

Same conic-gradient ring technique but restricted to Bitovi's brand palette: teal #00848B → light teal #00BEC6 → orange #F5532D → light orange #FF8A65 and back. Paired with a soft pulsing box-shadow. More on-brand than full rainbow; less "generic AI".

When to use: If the full rainbow feels too generic, this variant keeps the same rotating-ring AI grammar but in Bitovi's teal and orange. It reads as "branded AI tool" rather than just "any AI assistant".
The pulse: In addition to the ring, a soft box-shadow breathes in and out with a 3-second period. This makes the button look "alive" even when the page is idle and the user hasn't hovered yet.
4 Option C — Breathing Pulse (No Ring)

The quietest option: no rotating ring, just a calm breathing box-shadow. The teal glow pulses gently at idle. On hover, the border lights up teal and the shadow intensifies. Works well in contexts where a user is doing focused work and doesn't want a constantly spinning element.

Best for: Users in focused "flow" mode. The pulse is subtle enough not to pull attention, but noticeable enough that the user can find the button quickly when they need it.
Tradeoff: Less immediately recognizable as an AI tool — doesn't use the established "AI glow ring" pattern. Best paired with a clear tooltip or label on first use.
5 Option D — Dark Button with Shimmer & Inner Glow

A premium dark-theme variant: deep teal-black background (#0b1f21), light teal mascot, dark eyes, and a diagonal shimmer sweep on hover. Works well against both light and dark user pages — the dark button doesn't clash with white backgrounds and doesn't disappear against dark ones. Can be combined with any of the ring options above.

Disappear behavior: When the panel opens, the Eggburt button scale-fades to nothing (0.4s, ease-in). There's no "active/open" button state because there's no button — it vanished.

To close the panel, use the ✕ button inside the panel itself. This is a cleaner separation of "open" and "close" affordances.
Why disappear?
  • Removes visual clutter from the page
  • Avoids the "button fights with panel" problem
  • The panel is the intended focus once open
  • Re-opening is always intentional (close panel first)
